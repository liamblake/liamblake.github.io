Teaching

This pages details my teaching experience. For an overview of my industry and volunteering experience, please see my Linkedin profile.

I have been closely involved with Haide College, a joint initiative between the University of Adelaide and the Ocean University of China. This has involved on-site teaching in the lovely city of Qingdao, Shandong.

I have assisted with the delivery of:

  • Computational Bayesian Statistics: a fourth year course covering Bayesian modelling approaches with an emphasis on computational techniques.

  • Data Science: a third year course covering the mathematical formulation and implementation of modelling approaches in data science.

  • Decision Science: a third year course covering the modelling and simulation of stochastic systems.

  • Probability & Statistics: a second year course covering basic statistics and probability theory.

  • Projects in Mathematics: a second year course teaching students the fundamentals of mathematical writing, proof, and communication through a series of projects across both pure and applied mathematics.

  • Optimisation & Operations Research: a second year course covering techniques for linear optimisation.

  • Numerical Methods: a second year course covering numerical algorithms for interpolation, integration, differentiation, and equation solving.

  • Science Processes & Practices: a first year course that introduces students to critical thinking and scientific communication.

During my time at the University of Adelaide (now Adelaide University), I had the opportunity to work casually within the School of Computer and Mathematical Sciences as a lecturer, tutor and marker for a range of undergraduate courses. I have taught the following courses:

  • Decision Science PG: A postgraduate course covering simulation techniques in the support of decision making.
  • Capstone Project in Mathematics III: A third-year course that involves a small group of students working on an open-ended research project. I developed the project, lectures, practical material, and assessment for a group of 15 students and guided them throughout the semester.
  • Fluid Mechanics III: A third-year course covering fluid dynamics, including the derivation of physical laws and practical simulation methods.
  • Modelling with Ordinary Differential Equations III: A third-year course covering ordinary differential equations and their formulation, numerical solution, analysis, and application to a range of business and scientific settings.
  • Partial Differential Equations and Waves III: A third-year course covering partial differential equations, including formulation, analytical and numerical solutions, and interpretation.
  • Professional Practice III: A professional development course for third-year computer science and mathematics students, in which I worked closely with my students to develop their job application, interview, teamwork, and communication skills.
  • Statistical Modelling & Inference II: A second-year course covering statistical methods, including estimation, hypothesis esting, linear models, and maximum likelihood methods.
  • Mathematics IM, IA, and IB: A set of introductory first-year courses to calculus and linear algebra, taken by all mathematics and engineering students.
